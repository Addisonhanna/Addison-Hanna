About Me

I live in upstate South Carolina on a small working homestead. I generally love the natural sciences, particularly plants, animals, and geology. We have 40 chickens and grow a variety of fruit in our small orchard. Next year, we plan on adding goats, building a greenhouse, and creating a garden plot to cultivate a wider range of foods. I keep a few bonsai that are in training but have come a long way since starting the hobby a few years ago. I also have modest collection of indoor plants like orchids and philodendrons. Soon, I will have greater quantity and variety to keep once the greenhouse is finished. We are hoping to make it large enough to accomidate a turtle pond where we can keep our pet turtles and maybe even add a few more reptiles like a chameleon or tortoise. My work in pharmacy led me to seek knowledge about medicine's interactions with the body. I recently dove into medical studies, taking courses in human anatomy and physiology, psychology, and microbiology. I found the material to be incredibly interesting - the human body is far more complex than I had ever imagined prior to taking these courses. It was even more fascinating to see how all elements of physical science, such as chemistry and physics, are everpresent and control much of the body's functions. Seeing the gas laws appear in respiration, fluid dynamics in circulation, and properties of chemistry in digestion and the endocrine system highlighted the importance of a well-rounded education.
